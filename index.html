GAME OF THRONES

8 SEASONS | 73 EPISODES | TV-MA

Game of Thrones is HBO's epic fantasy drama based on George R.R. Martin's novels. Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, it follows noble families competing for the Iron Throne while facing a growing threat from the North. Known for its complex characters, political intrigue, and shocking plot twists across 8 seasons.

Based on : A Song of Ice and Fire

Created by : David Benioff D.B Weiss

Mother of Dragons

Daenerys Targaryen's evolution from exiled princess to the powerful Mother of Dragons showcases her quest to reclaim the Iron Throne. With her dragons and unwavering determination, she breaks chains across Essos before bringing her conquest to Westeros in a journey of fire and blood.

NIGHT'S WATCH

"Defenders of Giant Wall"

The Night's Watch is an ancient order dedicated to guarding the Wall, protecting the Seven Kingdoms from wildlings and White Walkers from the North. Their oath binds them for life, forsaking lands, titles, and family.

They wear only black, symbolizing their renunciation of past ties. Despite dwindling numbers, they remain steadfast in their duty to be "the shield that guards the realms of men."

HOUSE LANNISTER

"Hear Me Roar"

The wealthiest of the great houses, House Lannister of Casterly Rock is known for its gold mines and cunning leadership. The Lannisters played central roles in the War of the Five Kings through Tywin's strategic mind and Cersei's ruthless ambition.

Though their official motto is "Hear Me Roar," they're better known for the saying: "A Lannister always pays his debts," reflecting both their wealth and their tendency to settle scores.

HOUSE TYRELL

"Growing Strong"

House Tyrell of Highgarden rules over the Reach, the most fertile region in Westeros. Led by the shrewd Lady Olenna, they rose to prominence through political marriages and alliances rather than military might.

The Tyrells command the largest army in the realm and control much of the continent's food supply, making them invaluable allies and dangerous enemies in the game of thrones.

HOUSE TARGARYEN

"Fire And Blood"

The dragon-riding conquerors of Westeros, House Targaryen ruled the Seven Kingdoms for nearly 300 years. With their distinctive silver hair and purple eyes, they practiced dragon-taming and sometimes wed brother to sister to keep bloodlines pure.

Though nearly extinguished during Robert's Rebellion, Daenerys Targaryen's journey to reclaim her birthright and her three dragons reignited their legacy of fire and blood.

HOUSE STARK

"Winter is Coming"

The honorable rulers of the North, House Stark of Winterfell is one of the oldest lines of Westerosi nobility, descended from the First Men. Known for their unwavering sense of duty and justice, the Starks have endured despite countless tragedies.

Their motto, "Winter is Coming," reflects their practical nature and constant vigilance against the harsh realities of life. The direwolf on their sigil represents their strong connection to the North.

HOUSE BARATHEON

"Ours is the Fury"

Founded by Orys Baratheon, rumored to be the half-brother of Aegon the Conqueror, House Baratheon rules the Stormlands from their seat at Storm's End. Robert Baratheon's rebellion ended the Targaryen dynasty and placed him on the Iron Throne.

Known for their strength and fierce determination in battle, the Baratheons display a crowned stag on their banner, symbolizing power and nobility.

HOUSE ARRYN

"As High As Honor"

House Arryn rules the Vale from their impregnable fortress, the Eyrie, perched high in the Mountains of the Moon. As Wardens of the East, they command one of the most defensible regions in Westeros.

Their isolation has allowed them to stay neutral in many conflicts. The falcon and moon on their sigil represent their lofty position, while their words "As High As Honor" reflect both their mountainous home and their noble ideals.

LOVE STORY

Jon Snow and Daenerys Targaryen's complex relationship became one of the most tragic love stories in Game of Thrones. Beginning as allies, their connection deepened despite the looming threat of the White Walkers. The revelation of Jon's true identity as Aegon Targaryen, her nephew and rightful heir to the Iron Throne, complicated their romance. Their story ended tragically with Jon being forced to kill Daenerys after her destructive descent, fulfilling the bittersweet prophecy that has defined their intertwined destinies.
